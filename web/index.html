Order of Procedure how the tribunal sits
I

Seal

Each party encrypts evidence client-side and posts a confidential bond. Only a hash commitment reaches the chain.

inco lightning · xchacha20
II

Release

Once both sides are funded and sealed, Inco releases the decryption key to the discovery worker alone, never to a counterparty.

inco threshold gate
III

Convene

The worker decrypts and convenes the tribunal. The seats reason over plaintext in a nondet block and put one ruling to a roll call.

genlayer ic · nondet
IV

Enter

Only the verdict returns. The escrow splits the confidential pot by basis points without ever revealing its size.

verdict → escrow
A Note on the Limits the honest edge

Confidentiality holds against the public record and against the opposing party. It does not hold against the committee itself, which sees plaintext for the duration of a single call.

In place today: ephemeral keys discarded after the holding, seats that emit only the verdict, and slashing for leakage. Full confidentiality from the committee is the frontier, and waits on confidential compute at the node layer. We state this plainly rather than paper over it.
